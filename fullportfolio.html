Summary

SmartCaptions This was a team project that I worked on as a part of HackMIT. Have you ever listened in on a conversation and had no clue what was being talked about? Most of us have. So we sought to find a way to give people easy access to resources to educate them on a subject while listening to it. The basic premise behind our project was using text-to-speech translators and natural language processing to pick out the the most important words from the speech. A transcript would be developed with those words highlighted. If you clicked on one of those keywords a list of resources would appear to give you insight on the topic.

Summary

If you watched the video above, how many LEDs do think were used to create the display. The answer is not intuitive. In fact, when I first saw a similar project on Youtube, I was way off. The answer: eight. How is this possible? Persistence of vision. In other words, the brain can only process visual information so fast. So if you flash the LEDs faster than that, you won't even be able to recognize that they're flashing. To take advantage of this, you can move them while they're flashing and make cool designs. Or spell words. Your brain won't tell the difference between the moving LEDs and 60 stationary ones. I thought this was really cool, and was inspired to build this project. click here for more...
